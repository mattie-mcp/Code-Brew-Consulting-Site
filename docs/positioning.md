# Positioning & Messaging

> Owner: Brand & Positioning Strategist. See [`../PROJECT.md`](../PROJECT.md).

## The core narrative

> A senior engineering leader who builds and ships real systems — and the teams
> that own them. Codebrew Consulting is the proof: independent, end-to-end delivery
> for real clients.

EM-leaning means: **lead with leadership and outcomes, immediately back it with
technical depth.** A hiring manager should believe both "this person can run a team"
and "this person can still go deep" within the first scroll.

## Audiences (in priority order)

1. **Hiring managers / Directors / VPs of Eng** — scanning for: scope owned, team
   size led, business impact, technical judgment. Want signal fast.
2. **Technical recruiters / sourcers** — scanning for: title fit, tech keywords,
   availability, location/remote, easy contact path.
3. **Peers & references** — corroborate credibility; may share the link.

## The 10-second test

Above the fold, a recruiter must learn: **who you are, the level you operate at,
that you're open to roles, and how to reach you.** Everything else is depth on demand.

## Positioning of Codebrew

- Frame as a **vehicle for independent delivery**, not a company seeking clients.
- Use it to evidence: ownership without a safety net, breadth across the stack,
  client communication, shipping under real constraints.
- Avoid: pricing, "services" menus, "let's work together" agency CTAs, multiple
  fake team members. One line that signals "this is how I do independent work."

## Voice & tone

- Confident, specific, low-ego. Outcomes over adjectives.
- Quantify wherever possible (latency, cost, scale, team size, revenue, uptime).
- Plain language; no buzzword soup. Senior readers trust concrete detail.
- First person ("I led…", "I built…"), past-tense, active voice.

## Differentiators to surface (from resume)

- **The both/and, proven on the same job.** At BiggerPockets he was simultaneously
  the org's first eng leader *and* a hands-on staff engineer/architect — not a former
  IC who stopped coding.
- **Transformation operator.** Took a resistant org to ~80% AI adoption in four
  months; redesigned the SDLC; rebuilt the product–engineering relationship. Concrete,
  recent, measurable leadership outcomes.
- **AI-engineering depth that's actually shipped.** Claude Code standards, Figma MCP
  design-to-code, a production RAG search (Pinecone + GPT-4o + SSE) — this is a strong,
  timely hook and should be prominent.
- **Independent delivery (Codebrew).** Shipped a custom ERP-integrated plant-floor
  platform end-to-end — proof he owns outcomes without a company's scaffolding.
- **Range:** financial systems (Ellevest), connected products/IoT at scale (OST),
  consumer web (BiggerPockets), enterprise integration (Codebrew/.NET).

## Signature line

> "Staff engineer turned engineering manager — leads teams through transformation,
> and still ships." Anchor the AI-engineering work as the proof he's current.

## Messaging guardrails

- Don't dilute the FTE intent — the site is for getting hired, not booking clients.
- Don't overclaim; every quantified claim should survive a reference check.
- Keep consulting subordinate to the personal brand throughout.
